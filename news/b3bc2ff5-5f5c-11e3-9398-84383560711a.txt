                       (Close): US stocks jumped on Friday following the release of stronger-than-expected jobs figures. 
         The US economy created 203,000 jobs last month, figures showed, and the unemployment rate fell to a five-year low of 7%. 
         In response, the  Dow Jones   closed up 197.92 points 1.25% to 16,019.43. 
         The  S P 500  rose 1989 1.11% to 1,804.92, while the tech-rich  Nasdaq Composite  Index jumped 29.34 0.73% to 4,062.50. 
         Separate economic data showed that consumer spending rose by 0.3% in October, after increasing 0.2% the month before. 
         All but one of the shares in the Dow Jones traded higher, with the biggest risers being Intel, up 2.27%, and Boeing, up 2.03%. 
         Visa  was the only faller - losing less than 0.1%.
