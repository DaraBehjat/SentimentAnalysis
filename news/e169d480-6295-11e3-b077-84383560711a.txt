


PARIS  Dec 11 (Reuters) - French airline Air Caraibes
announced plans for a $1 billion order for three Airbus 
A350-1000 passenger jets and said it would lease a further three
smaller A350s in the largest fleet acquisition in its 10-year
history. The airline, which competes with Corsair and Air France
 on Caribbean destinations, said the leasing part of
the deal would involve three A350-900 aircraft to be rented from
U.S.-based International Lease Finance Corp.
