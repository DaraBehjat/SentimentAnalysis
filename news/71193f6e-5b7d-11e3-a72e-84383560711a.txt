

MILAN  Dec 2 (Reuters) - New car sales in Italy, Europe's
fourth-largest car market, fell 4.5 percent in November from the
same month a year ago to 102,201 vehicles, Italy's transport
ministry said on Monday. 
European car sales fell to a 20-year low in the first half
of 2013, and are in their fifth year of decline. Fiat's share of the Italian market was 27.2 percent
in November, compared with 28.1 percent last month and down from
29.6 percent in November last year, according to calculations
made by Reuters.
